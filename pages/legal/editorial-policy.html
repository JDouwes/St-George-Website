Editorial policy

Last reviewed by Julian Douwes M.D., Chief Medical Officer

We believe that patients researching Lyme disease treatment deserve accurate, transparent, and carefully reviewed information. This page explains who creates our content, how it is reviewed, and the editorial standards we hold ourselves to.

Our commitment: Every piece of medical content published on Lyme Treatment Germany is developed by the clinical team at Klinik St. Georg and reviewed by Julian Douwes M.D., Chief Medical Officer. We distinguish clearly between published research evidence and clinical experience, and we never make claims that go beyond what the evidence supports.

1. Who creates our content

Content on this website is developed by the medical team at Klinik St. Georg (St. George Hospital) in Bad Aibling, Germany. The team brings over 30 years of institutional experience treating Lyme disease, with more than 12,000 patients treated and over 30,000 hyperthermia sessions performed.

Content is written or reviewed by physicians with direct clinical experience in Lyme disease treatment, including specialists in infectious disease, integrative medicine, and hyperthermia therapy.

Medical review

All medical content is reviewed and approved by Julian Douwes M.D., Chief Medical Officer of Klinik St. Georg. Dr. Douwes oversees editorial accuracy and ensures that content reflects current clinical practice, published research, and appropriate medical standards.

2. Our evidence-based approach

We take a structured approach to the relationship between evidence and the claims we make:

Published research

Where peer-reviewed studies, systematic reviews, or established medical literature support a claim, we cite the source. For example, the thermolability of Borrelia bacteria is supported by published laboratory research including Reisinger et al. (1996, Scandinavian Journal of Infectious Diseases). We reference this work directly and provide sufficient context for readers to locate the original research.

Clinical experience

Some aspects of our treatment approach are based on decades of clinical experience rather than large-scale randomized controlled trials. When this is the case, we say so explicitly. We do not present clinical experience as though it carries the same evidentiary weight as a randomized controlled trial. We believe being transparent about this distinction builds trust rather than undermining it.

Investigational therapies

For therapies that are considered investigational, experimental, or not yet part of mainstream treatment guidelines, we use conservative language. We describe the therapy, explain the rationale and available evidence, and clearly note its current status. We never claim that an investigational therapy is a proven cure.

3. Claims we do not make

To maintain editorial integrity, we adhere to the following principles:

  • No cure claims. We never guarantee outcomes, promise remission, or state that any treatment will cure Lyme disease. Treatment outcomes vary between individuals, and we present realistic expectations.
  • No fear-based messaging. We describe the seriousness of Lyme disease honestly, without using language designed to exploit anxiety or create urgency beyond what is medically warranted.
  • No misleading comparisons. When we compare treatment approaches, we present factual differences and clearly distinguish between published evidence, institutional data, and editorial assessment.
  • No fabricated statistics. All numerical claims (patient counts, session numbers, outcomes data) are based on actual institutional records.

4. Patient stories and testimonials

When we share patient experiences, we present them as individual accounts. Each patient's journey is unique, and one person's outcome does not predict another's. Patient stories are shared with consent and are not edited to exaggerate positive outcomes or omit challenges.

We do not present individual patient experiences as generalizable evidence for treatment effectiveness.

5. Comparison content methodology

Our comparison pages (such as hyperthermia vs. antibiotics, or Germany vs. other countries) follow a transparent methodology:

  • Factual criteria: We compare based on measurable, verifiable factors such as treatment duration, mechanism of action, side effect profiles, and regulatory status
  • Fair representation: We present the strengths and limitations of all approaches, including our own
  • Labeled opinions: Where a comparison involves editorial judgment rather than fact, we label it clearly (for example, "Editorial assessment" or "Our perspective")
  • Disclosure: Every comparison page notes our relationship with Klinik St. Georg, so readers understand the perspective from which we write

6. Partnership disclosure

Lyme Treatment Germany is created in partnership with Klinik St. Georg. We are transparent about this relationship throughout the website. Our partnership disclosure page explains the nature of this relationship in detail.

This partnership means our content is informed by real clinical experience. It also means we have a perspective. We believe disclosing this openly is more trustworthy than pretending to be a neutral, unaffiliated information source.

7. Update frequency and review cycle

We review and update our content on an ongoing basis:

  • Clinical content (treatment pages, mechanism explanations, eligibility criteria) is reviewed at least every six months, or sooner when new research is published
  • General informational content (knowledge base articles, symptom guides) is reviewed annually
  • Practical content (costs, logistics, travel information) is updated whenever relevant changes occur
  • Every content page displays the date it was last reviewed

8. Reporting errors

If you find information on this website that you believe is inaccurate, outdated, or misleading, we want to hear from you. We take error reports seriously and will review and correct confirmed inaccuracies promptly.
