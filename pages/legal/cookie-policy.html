Cookie policy

This page explains what cookies are, which cookies this website uses, and how you can manage or disable them. We have written this in plain language so you can make informed decisions about your privacy.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit them. They allow the website to remember your actions and preferences over time, so you don't have to re-enter them whenever you return or move between pages.

Not all cookies are the same. Some are essential for a website to function. Others help website operators understand how visitors use the site, so they can improve it.

2. Cookies we use

We keep our cookie usage to a minimum. We do not use advertising, remarketing, or social media tracking cookies. The cookies active on this website fall into the following categories:

2.1 Essential cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function correctly. They cannot be switched off in our systems because they support core functionality.

Cookie Purpose Duration
cookie_consent Stores your cookie consent preference so the banner is not shown repeatedly 12 months
localStorage (quiz progress) Saves your progress in the symptom assessment quiz so you can return to it later. No personal health data is transmitted to our servers. Persistent until cleared

2.2 Analytics cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors navigate the website, which pages are most visited, and where visitors come from. This helps us improve our content and user experience. Analytics cookies are only set after you have given consent through our cookie banner.

Cookie Purpose Duration
_ga Google Analytics: distinguishes unique visitors 2 years
_ga_* Google Analytics: maintains session state 2 years

We have configured Google Analytics with the following privacy protections:

  • IP anonymization is enabled
  • Data sharing with Google is disabled
  • Advertising features are turned off
  • Data retention is set to 14 months

2.3 Advertising cookies

We do not use advertising cookies. We do not run remarketing campaigns, display ads, or share data with advertising networks. No cookies on this website are used for the purpose of targeted advertising.

3. Third-party cookies

Some elements on this website are provided by third parties. These services may set their own cookies:

  • Google Fonts: We load the Lora and DM Sans typefaces from Google's font service. Google may log basic connection information (IP address, browser type) when fonts are requested. Google states that font requests are not used for tracking or advertising purposes. See Google Fonts Privacy FAQ.
  • Google Analytics: As described above, analytics cookies are set by Google only after you consent. See Google Privacy Policy.
  • Kit (ConvertKit): If you subscribe to one of our free educational guides, the embedded Kit form may set a session cookie to process the form submission. See Kit Privacy Policy.

4. How to manage cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View which cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete individual cookies or all cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is being set

Here are links to cookie management instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Please note that disabling essential cookies may affect the functionality of certain features, such as the symptom assessment quiz progress saving.

5. Cookie consent banner

When you first visit our website, a cookie consent banner is displayed. This banner gives you the choice to accept or decline non-essential cookies (analytics). Essential cookies are always active because they are required for basic website functionality, as permitted under Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR.

Your consent preference is stored in a cookie (cookie_consent) so that the banner is not shown on every page load. You can change your preference at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site.

6. Local storage

In addition to cookies, we use your browser's localStorage to save progress in the symptom assessment quiz. This data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to our servers. It contains no personally identifiable information. You can clear localStorage through your browser's developer tools or settings.
