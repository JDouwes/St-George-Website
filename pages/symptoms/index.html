Symptom categories

Common Lyme disease symptoms

🧠

Neurological

Brain fog and cognitive dysfunction

Difficulty concentrating, word-finding problems, short-term memory loss, mental fatigue, and a feeling of thinking through cotton wool. These neurological symptoms are among the most common and most distressing features of Lyme disease, affecting daily function, work performance, and relationships.

⚡

Systemic

Chronic fatigue

Not ordinary tiredness — a bone-deep exhaustion that is not relieved by rest. Lyme-related fatigue often includes post-exertional malaise, where even minor activity leads to disproportionate crashes lasting hours or days. Many patients describe it as the single most debilitating symptom they experience.

Understand fatigue →
🦿

Musculoskeletal

Joint pain

Migratory joint pain that shifts between knees, shoulders, hips, and smaller joints — often without visible inflammation. Lyme arthritis can appear months or years after the initial infection and may be mistaken for rheumatoid arthritis, fibromyalgia, or age-related wear. The shifting pattern is a hallmark of Lyme involvement.

Understand joint pain →
🔥

Neurological

Nerve pain and neuropathy

Tingling, burning, numbness, or shooting pain — especially in the hands and feet. Lyme neuroborreliosis can affect peripheral nerves, cranial nerves (including facial palsy), and the central nervous system. These symptoms are often misattributed to carpal tunnel syndrome, multiple sclerosis, or idiopathic neuropathy.

Understand nerve pain →
❤️

Cardiovascular

Heart symptoms

Palpitations, chest pressure, shortness of breath, and exercise intolerance. Lyme carditis can cause conduction abnormalities including heart block. While severe cardiac involvement is less common, milder symptoms like tachycardia, irregular heartbeat, and reduced exercise tolerance affect a meaningful proportion of Lyme patients.

Understand heart symptoms →
🌙

Sleep

Sleep problems

Difficulty falling asleep, frequent waking, unrefreshing sleep, and disrupted circadian rhythm. Lyme disease can directly affect the neurological pathways that regulate sleep architecture. Poor sleep then amplifies every other symptom — fatigue, pain, cognitive dysfunction — creating a cycle that is difficult to break without targeted treatment.

Important context

Why Lyme symptoms are so often missed

Lyme disease is a multi-system infection that can mimic dozens of other conditions. The bacteria — Borrelia burgdorferi and related species — can affect the nervous system, the joints, the heart, the skin, and the immune system simultaneously. This is why patients often see multiple specialists, receive multiple diagnoses, and still feel no better.

The symptom pattern matters. When multiple seemingly unrelated symptoms appear together — fatigue and brain fog and joint pain and sleep disruption — the combination itself is diagnostically meaningful. If this pattern sounds familiar, we encourage you to take our symptom assessment or read our detailed guide on what Lyme disease is and how it affects the body.
