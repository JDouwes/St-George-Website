Language support for international patients

English is the primary language for international patient care at Klinik St. Georg. Here is what to expect regarding language and communication during every stage of your treatment journey.

Quick overview

You will not face a language barrier. Julian Douwes M.D. and key medical staff are fluent English speakers. All consultations, treatment discussions, and medical documentation for international patients are conducted and provided in English. From your first enquiry through video consultation, treatment, discharge, and follow-up, English is the working language.

English-language care throughout your journey

For international patients, English is the primary working language at every stage of the treatment process. Here is exactly what that covers.

English is used for

  • Initial enquiry and case review correspondence
  • Video consultations before your trip
  • Admission and orientation on arrival
  • All medical consultations with your treating physician
  • Treatment explanations and informed consent discussions
  • Daily check-ins and medical updates during your stay
  • Diagnostic reports and test results
  • Discharge summary and follow-up recommendations
  • Post-treatment video consultations
  • Communication with your physician at home

English-speaking medical team

The physicians who treat international Lyme patients at Klinik St. Georg are fluent in English. This is not interpreted care or care through a translator—your doctor speaks directly to you in your language.

Key medical staff

  • Julian Douwes M.D. — Chief Medical Officer. Native-level English speaker. Leads the international Lyme programme and is your primary point of contact for case reviews and follow-up.
  • International patient coordination team — English-speaking staff who manage scheduling, travel logistics, visa documentation, and day-to-day communication during your stay.
  • Nursing staff — key nursing staff working with international patients speak conversational to fluent English.

Because your physician speaks your language directly, nothing is lost in translation. Medical discussions about your diagnosis, treatment options, risks, and expectations are conducted with full nuance and clarity.

Documentation in English

The German medical system produces documentation in German by default. For international patients, all relevant documentation is provided in English.

Diagnostic reports

Blood work results, co-infection screening, immune panels, and other diagnostic findings provided in English with clear explanations.

Treatment records

Detailed record of all treatments administered, including hyperthermia sessions, medications, and integrative therapies.

Discharge summary

Comprehensive summary of your diagnosis, treatment course, clinical response, and prognosis, written for your physician at home.

Follow-up plan

Specific recommendations for monitoring, testing, and follow-up care, with timelines and criteria clearly stated in English.

These English-language documents are designed to be directly usable by your physician at home, regardless of which country you return to. No additional translation is needed.

Video consultations in English

Video consultations are available in English at every stage.

When video consultations are used

  • Before your trip — initial consultation to discuss your case, treatment options, and suitability for the programme
  • Pre-treatment preparation — review of your medical records, discussion of what to expect, and any questions you have
  • After treatment — follow-up consultations to review your progress, discuss test results, and adjust recommendations
  • Ongoing support — additional consultations as needed during your recovery period

Video consultations are scheduled at times that work for your time zone, whether you are in North America, Europe, Australia, or the Middle East.

Support for other languages

While English is the primary language for international patient care, the hospital can provide support for patients who speak other languages.

Additional language capabilities

  • German — the primary language of the hospital; all staff speak German
  • English — fluent among key medical and coordination staff
  • Other European languages — some staff members speak additional European languages; availability varies
  • Translation services — professional medical translation can be arranged for patients who require support in languages beyond English and German

If you are concerned about language and your primary language is not English or German, please mention this when you submit your initial enquiry. We will assess what support we can provide and be transparent about any limitations.

Language in daily life at the hospital

A few practical notes about navigating language during your stay beyond the medical context.

What to expect

  • Meals — dietary needs can be communicated in English to the catering team through your patient coordinator
  • Day-to-day requests — your international patient coordinator is available for any communication needs during your stay
  • Local navigation — Bad Aibling is a small Bavarian town; some local residents speak English, but proficiency varies. Basic German phrases are appreciated but not expected
  • Emergency communication — 24/7 English-language support is available through the hospital for any urgent needs
