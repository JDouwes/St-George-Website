Lyme treatment in Germany for UK patients

Practical information for British patients considering treatment at Klinik St. Georg. Short flights, post-Brexit travel, NHS considerations, costs, and what to expect.

Quick overview

UK citizens can travel to Germany visa-free for up to 90 days. Direct flights from London to Munich take under 2 hours. Klinik St. Georg in Bad Aibling is approximately one hour from Munich Airport. The short travel distance, cultural familiarity, and no significant timezone difference make Germany one of the most accessible international treatment destinations for British patients.

Getting to Germany from the UK

Germany is one of the closest and most accessible destinations for UK patients seeking Lyme treatment abroad. The journey is significantly shorter and less disruptive than traveling to clinics in North America or other regions.

Post-Brexit travel

Since Brexit, UK citizens no longer have freedom of movement within the EU. However, British passport holders can still enter Germany and the Schengen Area for up to 90 days within any 180-day period without a visa. No special medical visa is required. Your passport must be valid for at least three months beyond your planned departure and have been issued within the previous ten years.

Flights to Munich

Direct flights from UK airports

  • London Heathrow (LHR) — 1 hour 50 minutes. British Airways, Lufthansa. Multiple daily departures.
  • London Gatwick (LGW) — 1 hour 50 minutes. easyJet, Eurowings.
  • Manchester (MAN) — 2 hours. Lufthansa, easyJet.
  • Edinburgh (EDI) — 2 hours 15 minutes. easyJet, Lufthansa (seasonal).

Typical return fare: £80–£300 depending on airline, season, and booking lead time. Budget airlines often offer fares under £100.

Munich to Bad Aibling

From Munich Airport (MUC), Bad Aibling is approximately 80 km southeast. The journey takes about one hour by car or taxi. Alternatively, the train from Munich to Bad Aibling takes about one hour with a connection in Rosenheim. The hospital can arrange airport pickup.

No timezone adjustment needed

Germany is just one hour ahead of the UK (GMT+1 / CET). This means no jet lag, no sleep disruption, and no adjustment period. You can arrive in the morning and begin settling in the same day.

NHS, private care, and Germany

Understanding how Lyme treatment works within the UK healthcare system helps explain why some British patients look abroad.

NHS pathway for Lyme disease

The NHS provides antibiotic treatment for Lyme disease, typically a 21-day course of doxycycline for early-stage cases. NHS guidelines follow NICE recommendations, which are broadly aligned with conventional infectious disease approaches. For patients with persistent symptoms after standard treatment, the NHS pathway can become frustrating—referral times are long, specialist access is limited, and treatment options beyond repeat antibiotic courses are generally not available.

Private care in the UK

Private Lyme treatment is available through Lyme-literate practitioners in the UK, though the number of specialists is limited compared to the US. Private testing through laboratories and private consultations can address diagnostic gaps, but treatment modalities like whole-body hyperthermia and therapeutic apheresis are not available in the UK.

Why UK patients consider Germany

  • Treatment modalities not available in the UK (hyperthermia, apheresis, integrative protocols)
  • Short, manageable flight under 2 hours
  • No significant timezone adjustment
  • Cultural familiarity—Germany and the UK share a similar standard of medical care and patient expectations
  • English widely spoken in German medical settings, particularly at clinics experienced with international patients
  • Defined treatment timeline (2–3 weeks) compared to months of fragmented private care

Insurance and reimbursement

The NHS does not cover treatment abroad in most circumstances. Private UK health insurance policies vary—some international or comprehensive policies may provide partial coverage for planned treatment abroad. Check your policy terms and contact your insurer before traveling. Klinik St. Georg provides comprehensive medical documentation in English to support any reimbursement claims.

UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC)

The GHIC covers emergency and medically necessary treatment in EU countries, but it does not cover planned private medical treatment. Your treatment at Klinik St. Georg would not be covered by the GHIC.

Coordinating with your UK doctor

Before you travel

  • Inform your GP that you are seeking treatment in Germany for Lyme disease
  • Request a summary of your medical records, including test results and treatment history
  • Ask your GP if they are willing to coordinate follow-up care based on the German medical team's recommendations
  • If you have a private Lyme specialist, share their records as well—the more clinical history available, the better your treatment plan

After you return

  • You will receive a detailed discharge summary in English for your GP and any private specialists
  • Klinik St. Georg's team can coordinate directly with your UK physician if both parties consent
  • Follow-up blood tests and monitoring can be arranged through your GP or private laboratory
  • Remote video consultations with your German medical team are available for ongoing follow-up

Cultural notes for British patients

Many UK patients are pleasantly surprised by how familiar and comfortable Germany feels. A few practical notes that may be helpful.

  • Medical standards—German hospitals operate to the same high standards you would expect from the best UK facilities. Equipment is modern, hygiene protocols are rigorous, and patient care is thorough.
  • English proficiency—English is widely spoken in Bavarian hospitals and tourist areas. You will not face meaningful language barriers.
  • Food and comfort—hospital meals are wholesome German cuisine. Special dietary requirements are accommodated. Tea is available, though you may need to bring your preferred brand.
  • Payment—Germany is still more cash-oriented than the UK, though card payments are widely accepted. Bring a small amount of euros for local purchases.
  • Driving—if your companion plans to hire a car, UK driving licences are valid in Germany. Remember to drive on the right.
  • Bad Aibling—a charming Bavarian spa town at the foot of the Alps. Your companion will find pleasant walks, good restaurants, and genuine Bavarian hospitality.

What to bring

Essential documents

  • Valid UK passport (valid for at least 3 months beyond your stay; issued within the last 10 years)
  • Complete medical records and test results
  • Current medication list with dosages and generic names
  • Insurance documentation (if applicable)
  • Printed confirmation of your treatment schedule
  • GHIC card (for any emergency situations outside of your planned treatment)

Practical items

  • European power adapter (Type C/F; UK three-pin plugs will not work without one)
  • Comfortable clothing for a hospital stay
  • Laptop, tablet, or books for downtime between treatment sessions
  • Your own toiletries and comfort items
  • Sufficient supply of any current medications for your entire trip
  • Layers—Bavaria can be cool even in summer, and delightfully warm in autumn
