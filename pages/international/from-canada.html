Lyme treatment in Germany for Canadian patients

Practical information for Canadian patients considering treatment at Klinik St. Georg. Healthcare system limitations, flights, provincial insurance, cost expectations, and how to coordinate with your doctor at home.

Quick overview

Canadian citizens can travel to Germany visa-free for up to 90 days. Direct flights from Toronto and Montreal to Munich take 8–9 hours. Provincial health insurance (OHIP, MSP, RAMQ, etc.) does not cover planned treatment abroad, so this will be a self-pay programme. Klinik St. Georg provides English-language care and will coordinate with your Canadian physician for follow-up.

Lyme disease in the Canadian healthcare system

Canada faces a growing Lyme disease burden as tick populations expand northward with climate change. Despite increasing incidence, chronic Lyme patients face significant barriers within the Canadian healthcare system.

Common challenges for Canadian Lyme patients

  • Limited recognition of chronic Lyme — many Canadian physicians follow guidelines that do not recognise persistent infection after standard treatment
  • Diagnostic limitations — standard two-tier testing through provincial labs can miss late-stage or chronic infections
  • Specialist wait times — referrals to infectious disease specialists can take months in many provinces
  • No access to advanced modalities — whole-body hyperthermia, therapeutic apheresis, and comprehensive integrative Lyme programmes are not available in Canada
  • Limited treatment duration — standard antibiotic courses are typically restricted to 14–28 days
  • Provincial variation — access to Lyme-knowledgeable physicians varies significantly by province

The Canadian Lyme Disease Foundation and patient advocacy groups have worked to improve awareness, but systemic barriers remain for patients with chronic symptoms.

Getting to Germany

Flights to Munich

Direct flights from major Canadian cities

  • Toronto (YYZ) — 8–9 hours direct. Lufthansa, Air Canada. Multiple daily departures.
  • Montreal (YUL) — 8 hours direct. Air Canada (seasonal), Lufthansa via Frankfurt.
  • Vancouver (YVR) — 10–11 hours direct. Lufthansa, Condor (seasonal). Alternatively via Frankfurt (12–14 hours total).
  • Calgary (YYC) — via Frankfurt or Toronto; approximately 12–14 hours total.
  • Ottawa (YOW) — via Toronto or Montreal; approximately 10–12 hours total.

Typical round-trip fare: CAD $900–$2,000 depending on origin, airline, and booking lead time.

Visa and entry

Canadian citizens do not need a visa to enter Germany. Under the Schengen Agreement, Canadians can stay in the Schengen Area for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. Your passport must be valid for at least three months beyond your planned departure. No medical visa is required.

Time zone

Germany is 6 hours ahead of Eastern Time (Toronto, Montreal, Ottawa) and 9 hours ahead of Pacific Time (Vancouver). Most patients adjust within 2–3 days. We recommend arriving a day before your scheduled programme start.

Insurance and cost

Provincial health insurance

Provincial health insurance plans (OHIP in Ontario, MSP in British Columbia, RAMQ in Quebec, etc.) do not cover planned medical treatment outside Canada. This treatment will be self-pay.

Private insurance

If you have private supplemental health insurance through your employer or individually, check whether international medical treatment is covered. Some policies provide partial coverage for planned medical care abroad, particularly when the treatment is not available domestically. Request pre-authorisation documentation before travelling.

Tax considerations

Medical treatment abroad may qualify as an eligible medical expense on your Canadian tax return. Medical travel expenses (including transportation and accommodation for the patient and, in some cases, a companion) may also be claimable. Consult with a Canadian tax professional about your specific circumstances.

Coordinating with your Canadian doctor

Before you travel

  • Request copies of your complete medical records, test results, and treatment history
  • Inform your family physician or specialist that you are considering treatment abroad
  • Ask if they are willing to coordinate on follow-up care based on the German team's recommendations
  • If your physician is unfamiliar with hyperthermia treatment, provide information from this website or request that Klinik St. Georg send a clinical summary

After you return

  • Comprehensive English-language discharge summary provided for your Canadian physician
  • Follow-up blood tests and monitoring can be arranged through your family doctor or walk-in lab services
  • Remote video consultations with your German medical team available for ongoing questions
  • Klinik St. Georg can coordinate directly with your Canadian physician if both parties consent
