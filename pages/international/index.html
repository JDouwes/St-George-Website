Your destination

Klinik St. Georg, Bad Aibling

Our hospital is located in Bad Aibling, a traditional Bavarian spa town in the foothills of the Alps. It is a quiet, green, restorative setting — ideal for treatment and recovery. The facility is a fully licensed 70-bed inpatient hospital with modern treatment rooms, private patient accommodation, and an experienced international patient support team.

We have been welcoming international Lyme patients since the mid-1990s. Our team understands the logistical, emotional, and financial considerations that come with traveling abroad for medical care — and we are here to help with every detail.

Getting here

How to reach Bad Aibling

By air

The nearest major airport is Munich International Airport (MUC), which receives direct flights from most major international cities including New York, London, Toronto, Sydney, Dubai, and Singapore. The airport is approximately 80 kilometers from Bad Aibling, and the journey takes about one hour by car or train.

Airport to hospital

We can arrange a private transfer from Munich airport directly to the hospital or to your accommodation in Bad Aibling. Alternatively, the German rail system (Deutsche Bahn) operates frequent trains from Munich Central Station to Bad Aibling station — the journey takes approximately 45 minutes and the station is a short taxi ride from the hospital.

By train

Bad Aibling is well connected to the German rail network. High-speed ICE trains from Berlin, Hamburg, Frankfurt, and other major German cities connect through Munich. From Munich, regional trains run to Bad Aibling approximately every 30 minutes.

By car

Bad Aibling is located just off the A8 motorway (Munich – Salzburg). Free parking is available at the hospital. If you are driving from Austria, the distance from Salzburg is approximately 100 kilometers.

Practical information

Everything you need to plan your stay

Accommodation

Where to stay

During your 2–3 week treatment, you will stay as an inpatient at the hospital in a private room. Accompanying family members can arrange accommodation at nearby hotels and guesthouses in Bad Aibling — a charming Bavarian town with plenty of comfortable options within walking distance. We provide a list of recommended accommodations upon booking.

Language

Communication support

Our medical team and support staff speak fluent English. All consultations, treatment discussions, and written materials are available in English. You will never feel lost in translation. For patients who speak other languages, we can arrange interpretation support for major languages upon request.

Visa

Entry requirements

Citizens of the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and most EU countries do not need a visa for stays under 90 days — which covers the typical treatment period. If you require a medical visa, we provide official invitation letters and treatment documentation to support your application. Our international coordinator can advise on requirements for your specific country.

Costs

Treatment investment

Every treatment program is different, so costs vary depending on your diagnostic needs and treatment plan. We provide a detailed, transparent cost estimate after your initial consultation — before you make any commitment. We also provide full documentation for insurance reimbursement claims. Many international patients have successfully obtained partial or full reimbursement from their insurers.

Support

International coordinator

From your first inquiry, you will have a dedicated international patient coordinator who helps with scheduling, travel logistics, documentation, and any questions before, during, and after your stay. You are never navigating this alone.

Follow-up

After you return home

Treatment does not end when you leave Germany. We provide a comprehensive follow-up plan, coordinate with your local physicians, and offer remote consultations to monitor your progress. You will have access to our medical team by phone and email for as long as you need us.
