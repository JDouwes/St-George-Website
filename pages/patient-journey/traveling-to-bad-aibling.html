Traveling to Bad Aibling

Getting to Klinik St. Georg is simpler than most patients expect. Here is everything you need to know about flights, trains, and arriving in this quiet Bavarian town.

Patients often describe the alpine setting as part of the healing. Bad Aibling sits in the foothills of the Bavarian Alps, surrounded by green meadows, forested hills, and views of the mountains. It is one of Germany's oldest spa towns—a place where people have come to heal for over a century.

Nearest airports

Two international airports serve Bad Aibling, both with excellent connections to North America, the UK, and the rest of Europe.

1

Munich Airport (MUC) — 45 minutes

Munich Airport is the primary gateway for most international patients. It is Germany's second-largest airport with direct flights from major cities worldwide, including New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, London, Dubai, and many others. From the airport, reaching Bad Aibling takes approximately 45 minutes by car or about 90 minutes by train with one connection.

2

Salzburg Airport (SZG) — 1 hour

Salzburg Airport in neighboring Austria is approximately one hour from Bad Aibling by car. It is smaller than Munich but convenient for patients connecting through European hubs such as London, Amsterdam, or Frankfurt. Some patients find it a quieter, less crowded arrival experience.

Train from Munich to Bad Aibling

Germany's rail network makes it easy to reach Bad Aibling without renting a car.

Regional trains run from München Hauptbahnhof (Munich Central Station) to Bad Aibling station. The direct journey takes approximately 50 minutes on the Meridian/BRB line toward Rosenheim. Trains depart frequently throughout the day, typically every 30 to 60 minutes.

The fare is approximately €15 one way. Tickets can be purchased at the station, through ticket machines, or via the Deutsche Bahn (DB) app, which has an English-language interface. From Bad Aibling station, the clinic is a short taxi ride of about five minutes.

Train travel tips

  • Look for trains toward Rosenheim departing from München Hbf (platforms clearly marked)
  • The Bayern-Ticket (Bavaria day pass) covers unlimited regional train travel for the day—good value if you are traveling with a companion
  • Munich Airport has its own S-Bahn (suburban rail) connection to München Hbf, taking about 40 minutes
  • Staff at German train stations are generally helpful and many speak English

Airport transfer options

You have several ways to get from the airport to the clinic.

1

Clinic-arranged transfer

The clinic can arrange a private car transfer from Munich or Salzburg airport directly to Klinik St. Georg. This is the most comfortable option, especially if you are arriving tired from a long flight. The driver will meet you at arrivals. Coordinate this through your international patient coordinator when confirming your treatment dates.

2

Taxi or private car

A taxi from Munich Airport to Bad Aibling costs approximately €100–€120 and takes about 45 minutes. From Salzburg Airport, expect roughly €120–€150. Ride-hailing services such as Uber are also available in Munich. Pre-booking is recommended, especially for early morning or late evening arrivals.

3

Train (most economical)

The train from Munich Airport via München Hbf to Bad Aibling costs approximately €15–€25 total and takes about 90 minutes including the connection. This is a comfortable and scenic option if you are feeling well enough and traveling light. The trains are clean, punctual, and easy to navigate.

About Bad Aibling

Bad Aibling is a small, traditional spa town in Upper Bavaria, located in the Mangfall valley between Munich and the Chiemsee—one of Germany's largest and most beautiful lakes. The town has a population of about 18,000 and has been recognized as a health resort (Kurort) since the 19th century.

What to expect

Bad Aibling is quiet, clean, and walkable. The town center has cafes, bakeries, a weekly market, pharmacies, and small shops. It is not a tourist hotspot, which is part of its appeal—there is a calm, unhurried quality that many patients find restorative.

The surrounding landscape is green and gently hilly, with the Bavarian Alps visible to the south. On clear days, the mountain panorama is remarkable. Walking paths along the Mangfall river and through the Kurpark (spa park) are accessible even on days when your energy is limited.

Climate

Upper Bavaria has a continental climate with warm summers and cold winters. Spring and autumn are mild but can be changeable—pack layers. Summer temperatures range from 20–28°C (68–82°F). Winter can be cold, with temperatures sometimes dropping below freezing and occasional snow, which gives the town a picturesque, alpine character.

Safety and practicality

Bad Aibling is extremely safe. Germany has universal healthcare infrastructure, well-maintained roads, reliable public transit, and high standards of civic order. English is widely understood, especially in medical settings and tourist-facing businesses. Credit cards are accepted in most places, though some smaller shops and restaurants prefer cash (euros).

Many patients arrive nervous about being in an unfamiliar place far from home. By the end of their first day, most feel a surprising sense of peace. The town is designed for healing—it has been doing this for over a hundred years.
