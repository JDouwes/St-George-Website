Preparing for treatment in Germany

A clear, practical checklist for everything you need to organize before your trip—so you can arrive focused on healing, not logistics.

Preparing for treatment abroad can feel overwhelming, especially when you are already managing a chronic illness. This page breaks everything down into manageable steps. Our international patient coordinator is also available to help with any part of this process.

Medical records to bring

The more complete your medical records, the more efficiently your treatment team can design your protocol. Gather what you can—but do not delay your trip if some records are unavailable.

Essential records

  • All Lyme disease test results: Western blot, ELISA, PCR, specialty lab panels (IGeneX, ArminLabs, etc.)
  • Co-infection testing results: Babesia, Bartonella, Ehrlichia, Anaplasma, Mycoplasma
  • Recent blood work: complete blood count, metabolic panel, inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR)
  • Imaging: MRI, CT, or ultrasound reports relevant to your symptoms
  • A chronological summary of your illness: when symptoms began, key milestones, what has changed over time
  • Complete treatment history: all antibiotics, supplements, and alternative therapies tried, with approximate dates and your response

Digital copies are fine. If you have records in a patient portal, download PDFs before your trip. The clinic can work with records in English or German. If your records are in another language, a brief summary translation is helpful but not required.

Medications and supplements

Bring with you

  • A complete, written list of all current medications with exact dosages and prescribing physician
  • Enough supply of each medication to cover your entire stay plus a few extra days
  • All medications in their original packaging with pharmacy labels
  • Current supplements with brand names and dosages
  • Any known drug allergies or adverse reactions documented

Keep medications in your carry-on luggage, not checked bags. If you take controlled substances, carry a letter from your prescribing physician explaining the medical necessity—this is important for customs in Germany.

Travel insurance

While your treatment costs are arranged directly with the clinic, travel insurance is strongly recommended to cover unexpected situations.

Insurance considerations

  • Travel medical insurance that covers emergency care in Germany
  • Trip cancellation coverage in case you need to reschedule due to illness
  • Confirm that your policy does not exclude pre-existing conditions or treatment-related travel
  • Keep your insurance documents accessible (digital and printed copies)
  • Note: treatment at Klinik St. Georg is arranged and paid separately from travel insurance

For guidance on insurance coverage for the treatment itself, see our insurance and payment page.

What to pack for 2–3 weeks

Pack for comfort and practicality. You are staying in a hospital, not a hotel, but the environment is comfortable and there is no strict dress code.

Clothing and comfort

  • Comfortable, loose-fitting clothes for treatment days and rest
  • Warm layers: the Bavarian climate can be cool, especially mornings and evenings
  • Comfortable walking shoes for the clinic grounds and short walks in town
  • Sleepwear and a robe or cardigan for your room
  • One outfit for dining out if you plan to visit local restaurants on good days

Practical essentials

  • Passport and travel documents (keep digital copies in your email as backup)
  • Phone charger and a European plug adapter (Type C or F)
  • Laptop or tablet for video calls and entertainment during rest periods
  • Headphones for music, audiobooks, or meditation during treatment
  • A journal or notebook for tracking symptoms and writing down questions
  • Any personal comfort items: a favorite pillow, blanket, or book

Pre-treatment preparation protocol

Your physician may recommend specific steps in the weeks before your arrival to help your body prepare for treatment.

1

Dietary preparation

In the two to four weeks before treatment, focus on an anti-inflammatory diet: reduce sugar, processed foods, and alcohol. Increase vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Staying well-hydrated is especially important. These steps help reduce your inflammatory baseline and support your body's ability to detoxify during treatment.

2

Supplement adjustments

Your physician may ask you to stop certain supplements or medications before arrival—particularly blood thinners or specific herbal preparations that could interfere with treatment. They will provide a clear list with timing instructions. Do not make changes without guidance from the clinic.

3

Detoxification support

Gentle detoxification preparation—such as increasing water intake, supporting liver function, and ensuring regular bowel movements—helps your body process the toxins released during treatment more efficiently. Your physician will provide specific recommendations based on your case.

4

Rest and stress reduction

Arrive as rested as possible. If you can, reduce commitments in the week before departure. Your body will respond better to treatment when it is not depleted by travel stress and last-minute preparations.

Master preparation checklist

Four weeks before

  • Confirm treatment dates with the clinic
  • Book flights and arrange travel insurance
  • Begin dietary preparation protocol
  • Request copies of all medical records

Two weeks before

  • Confirm medication supply covers your entire stay
  • Adjust supplements per physician instructions
  • Arrange accommodation for your companion if applicable
  • Download digital copies of medical records and travel documents

Day before departure

  • Medications in carry-on luggage with pharmacy labels
  • Medical records accessible (printed or digital)
  • Passport, insurance documents, and clinic contact information
  • European plug adapter packed
  • Clinic arrival instructions reviewed
