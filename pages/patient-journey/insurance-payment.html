Insurance and payment

We believe in complete financial transparency. Here is what treatment costs, what is included, and how patients typically manage payment.

We understand that the financial commitment of treatment abroad weighs heavily, especially after years of medical expenses. We provide detailed cost estimates before you commit, so you can make an informed decision without surprises. If the financial picture does not work, we respect that completely.

Self-pay: the most common path

The majority of international patients at Klinik St. Georg pay for treatment directly. This is standard for specialized medical care abroad, and the clinic's billing process is designed to be clear and straightforward.

Your cost estimate is provided after your initial consultation, once your physician has assessed your case and designed a personalized treatment plan. The estimate is detailed and itemized, so you know exactly what you are paying for.

Typical treatment costs

Treatment costs vary based on the complexity of your case, the duration of your stay, and the specific therapies included in your protocol. As a general guide:

Cost range

  • A comprehensive 2–3 week treatment program typically costs €15,000–€25,000
  • More complex cases requiring extended stays or additional procedures may be higher
  • Shorter, targeted follow-up cycles are typically less than the initial treatment
  • Your personalized estimate will reflect your specific needs, not a generic package price

These figures are provided as guidance. Your actual cost will be confirmed in writing before you commit to treatment. We do not add unexpected charges during your stay.

What is included in your treatment package

The treatment estimate covers a comprehensive package. You are not charged separately for each individual service.

Included in your treatment

  • Comprehensive diagnostic workup: blood panels, specialty Lyme and co-infection testing, imaging if indicated
  • Whole-body hyperthermia sessions with full medical monitoring and sedation
  • Intravenous antibiotic protocols tailored to your infection profile
  • Integrative therapies: ozone therapy, neural therapy, detoxification support, immune modulation
  • Daily physician consultations and ongoing monitoring
  • Private room with en-suite bathroom for the duration of your stay
  • Three meals daily with dietary accommodation
  • Discharge consultation with detailed follow-up plan
  • Post-treatment summary and documentation for your home physicians

Not typically included: travel costs, companion accommodation, and any personal expenses during your stay. These are arranged separately.

Insurance coverage options

While most patients self-pay, some insurance pathways may provide partial or full coverage.

International health insurance

Some international and expatriate health insurance policies cover medically necessary treatment abroad. Whether your treatment qualifies depends on your specific policy. We recommend contacting your insurer before your consultation and asking specifically whether treatment for Lyme disease at a licensed German hospital is covered under your plan. The clinic provides all necessary medical documentation to support insurance claims.

German private insurance (PKV)

Patients with German private health insurance (Private Krankenversicherung) may have partial or full coverage for treatment at Klinik St. Georg. Coverage depends on your specific policy terms and whether the proposed treatment is considered medically indicated. We work with German PKV insurers regularly and can assist with the pre-authorization process.

German statutory insurance (GKV)

German statutory health insurance generally does not cover the specialized treatment protocols offered at Klinik St. Georg for Lyme disease. However, individual exceptions may apply. We can advise on whether a coverage application is worth pursuing in your case.

Regardless of your insurance situation, the clinic provides comprehensive documentation of your treatment, including detailed invoices, medical reports, and a discharge summary. This documentation is essential for insurance reimbursement claims and tax deductions.

Payment methods and timing

1

Before treatment

A deposit is required to confirm your treatment dates. The amount and timing will be communicated clearly when you receive your cost estimate. The remaining balance is typically settled during or at the conclusion of your stay.

2

Accepted payment methods

The clinic accepts bank transfers (SEPA and international wire), major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard), and debit cards. Bank transfer is the most common method for international patients. The clinic provides banking details and can issue invoices in multiple currencies for reference, though payment is processed in euros.

3

Invoicing

You will receive a detailed, itemized invoice that meets international medical billing standards. This invoice is designed to be usable for insurance reimbursement claims in your home country. If your insurer requires a specific format or additional documentation, the billing team can accommodate that.

Medical expense tax deductions

Treatment costs for Lyme disease may be tax-deductible in your home country as a qualified medical expense. This can meaningfully reduce your net out-of-pocket cost.

For US patients

The IRS allows a medical expense deduction for treatment costs, including travel expenses, when the treatment is medically necessary and not available domestically in a comparable form. Under current tax law, medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of your adjusted gross income can be deducted on Schedule A. This may include the treatment itself, flights, accommodation, and meals during your medical stay. Consult a tax professional familiar with medical tourism deductions to maximize your benefit.

For patients in other countries

Many countries allow tax deductions or credits for medical expenses incurred abroad. The detailed invoice and medical documentation provided by the clinic are designed to support these claims. We recommend consulting a tax advisor in your country before or shortly after treatment.

The clinic does not provide tax advice, but our billing team can ensure your documentation meets the requirements of common international tax jurisdictions.
