After treatment: recovery and follow-up

Treatment does not end when you leave the clinic. Here is what to expect in the weeks and months ahead—and how we continue to support you.

Recovery from chronic Lyme disease is rarely instant. Most patients experience a gradual arc of improvement over weeks to months. Understanding this timeline helps you stay patient during the difficult days and recognize progress when it comes.

The first weeks after treatment

When you leave Klinik St. Georg, your body is still processing the effects of treatment. The first two to four weeks are a transition period.

Many patients feel tired during this period. This is not a sign that treatment did not work—your immune system is actively recovering and reorganizing. Rest is essential, not optional. Most physicians recommend limiting your activity, avoiding stress where possible, and giving your body the space it needs to heal.

Some patients notice improvements within the first week after returning home. Others experience a delayed response, where significant improvement begins four to eight weeks post-treatment. Both patterns are normal and well-documented in the clinic's experience with thousands of patients.

Common recovery patterns

There is no single recovery trajectory. However, three general patterns emerge across patients.

1

Gradual, steady improvement

Some patients experience a slow, consistent reduction in symptoms over weeks and months. Energy returns incrementally. Pain decreases. Cognitive clarity improves. This pattern is common and encouraging, though it requires patience—progress can be hard to see day-to-day but becomes clear when you compare where you are now to where you were a month ago.

2

Delayed response

Other patients feel little change in the first few weeks, then experience a notable shift—sometimes described as a turning point—around four to eight weeks post-treatment. The body needed time to process. If you are in this pattern, the waiting period can be difficult. Your follow-up team is there to reassure and guide you through it.

3

Fluctuating improvement

Many patients experience a wave pattern: good days followed by setback days, with the overall trend moving toward improvement. This can be emotionally challenging. Tracking your symptoms in a simple journal helps you see the broader trajectory when individual days feel discouraging.

Follow-up protocol

You are not on your own after discharge. The Klinik St. Georg team maintains an active follow-up relationship with every patient.

Follow-up schedule

  • Two-week post-discharge check-in: virtual consultation with your treating physician to assess early recovery
  • Six-week follow-up: review of symptoms, lab work if indicated, adjustment of home protocols
  • Three-month assessment: comprehensive evaluation of treatment response, discussion of next steps
  • Ongoing availability: the team can be reached by email for questions between scheduled check-ins

Follow-up consultations are conducted by video call, so you can participate from anywhere in the world. Your physician will review your progress, answer questions, and adjust your home protocol if needed. These are real clinical consultations, not administrative check-ins.

When to return for additional treatment

Some patients achieve significant improvement from a single treatment cycle. Others benefit from additional rounds—particularly patients with long-standing chronic Lyme, multiple co-infections, or significant immune compromise.

Your physician will discuss this with you during follow-up. A second treatment cycle, if recommended, is typically scheduled three to six months after the first. It is shorter and more targeted, building on the progress achieved during the initial stay.

The decision to return is always yours. There is no automatic enrollment or pressure to book additional treatment. Your physician will give you their honest clinical recommendation, and you decide.

Supporting recovery at home

Your discharge plan includes specific recommendations tailored to your case. These commonly include:

Home protocol elements

  • Continued oral antibiotics if prescribed, with clear duration and dosage instructions
  • Supplement protocol: targeted supplements to support immune function, detoxification, and recovery
  • Dietary guidance: anti-inflammatory nutrition to support ongoing healing
  • Activity recommendations: gradual return to exercise, starting with walking and gentle movement
  • Sleep hygiene: prioritizing quality rest as a cornerstone of recovery
  • Stress management: practical strategies to reduce immune-suppressing stress

Your physician will explain the rationale behind each recommendation so you understand not just what to do, but why it matters for your recovery.

When to contact the clinic urgently

Most post-treatment symptoms are expected and manageable. However, contact the clinic promptly if you experience:

Contact us if you experience

  • High fever (above 39°C / 102°F) that persists beyond 24 hours
  • Severe or worsening symptoms that do not respond to the management strategies provided
  • New neurological symptoms: sudden vision changes, severe headaches, numbness, or weakness
  • Signs of allergic reaction to prescribed medications
  • Significant emotional distress or mental health concerns

The clinic provides emergency contact information at discharge. You are never a burden for reaching out. If something feels wrong, trust your instinct and contact the team.

Recovery takes time, and patience with yourself is part of the process. The hardest part is often the uncertainty of not knowing how long it will take. But you have taken a significant step, and we are here to walk the rest of the road with you.
