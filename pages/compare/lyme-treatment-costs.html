Understanding Lyme treatment costs

A transparent breakdown of what treatment in Germany costs, what is included, what is not, and how it compares to the cumulative cost of chronic Lyme treatment elsewhere.

Quick overview

A comprehensive 2–3 week Lyme treatment programme at Klinik St. Georg typically costs €15,000–€25,000. This includes diagnostics, hyperthermia sessions, antibiotic therapy, integrative treatments, hospital accommodation, and meals. Additional costs include international flights and companion accommodation. A personalised cost estimate is provided after your initial consultation, before any commitment.

What is included in the treatment programme

The programme fee covers your complete inpatient stay. Here is what that includes.

Included in the programme fee

  • Comprehensive diagnostic workup (blood panels, co-infection screening, immune markers)
  • Whole-body hyperthermia sessions (number determined by your treatment plan)
  • Antibiotic therapy (IV and/or oral, tailored to your infection profile)
  • Integrative therapies (IV laser, photodynamic therapy, immune modulation)
  • H.E.L.P. apheresis sessions where clinically indicated
  • Daily medical oversight by your treating physician
  • Hospital accommodation in a private or semi-private room
  • All meals during your inpatient stay
  • English-language medical documentation and discharge summary
  • Post-treatment follow-up consultation plan

Not included in the programme fee

  • International flights to and from Munich
  • Accommodation for a travelling companion (hotels from €60–€120/night in Bad Aibling)
  • Pre-treatment laboratory tests at home (if requested before arrival)
  • Travel insurance
  • Personal expenses and incidentals
  • Airport transfers (can be arranged separately)

What determines your cost

The range of €15,000–€25,000 reflects the variability in individual treatment needs. Several factors influence where your programme falls within this range.

Factors that affect cost

  • Disease complexity — patients with multiple co-infections, neurological involvement, or severe immune dysfunction may require more intensive protocols
  • Number of hyperthermia sessions — typically 2–4 sessions, but some patients benefit from additional sessions
  • Programme duration — standard programmes are 2–3 weeks; complex cases may require extended stays
  • Diagnostic requirements — the breadth of testing needed depends on your prior diagnostic history
  • Apheresis sessions — H.E.L.P. apheresis is included when clinically indicated but adds to cost if multiple sessions are needed
You will receive a detailed, personalised cost estimate after your initial consultation. This estimate is provided before you make any commitment or travel arrangements. There are no hidden fees.

Many patients who come to Germany have already spent $30,000–$80,000 or more on Lyme treatment in the US over multiple years. For these patients, the German programme represents a concentrated investment in a different clinical approach, not an additional expense on top of everything else.

Insurance and financial considerations

Health insurance

Most standard health insurance plans (US, UK, Canadian, Australian) do not cover planned treatment abroad. However, several avenues are worth exploring.

Insurance options to investigate

  • International health insurance — some global policies cover planned medical treatment abroad with pre-authorisation
  • Supplemental insurance — some policies provide out-of-network or international coverage
  • Reimbursement claims — the hospital provides detailed English-language documentation for insurance claims; some patients have received partial reimbursement
  • HSA/FSA (US patients) — medical treatment abroad may qualify as a deductible medical expense; consult your tax professional
  • Medical tourism tax deductions — in some jurisdictions, medical travel expenses may be tax-deductible when the treatment is not available locally

Payment structure

Payment arrangements are discussed after your initial consultation. A deposit is typically required to secure your treatment dates, with the balance due during your stay. The hospital accepts bank transfers and major credit cards.

Why cost is not the only consideration

We understand that €15,000–€25,000 is a significant investment. We are not in the business of minimising that reality. But we believe it is important to consider cost in context.

What you are investing in

  • 30 years of specialised experience — Klinik St. Georg has treated over 12,000 Lyme patients since 1994
  • Multi-modal treatment — hyperthermia, antibiotics, apheresis, and integrative therapies coordinated by one team
  • Hospital-grade medical oversight — daily monitoring by physicians, not periodic outpatient visits
  • A defined timeline — 2–3 weeks with a clear beginning and end, rather than open-ended monthly appointments
  • Comprehensive documentation — detailed discharge summary and follow-up plan for your physician at home

The cheapest option is not always the best option. What matters is whether the treatment addresses your specific clinical needs effectively, and whether the team delivering it has the experience and tools to do so.
